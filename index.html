About

It's hard to visualize a world of billions. Studies have shown that we have a cognitive limit to the number of stable, meaningful relationships we can maintain. Although this number varies from person to person, our relationship limit is roughly capped at 150 individuals. When trying to comprehend the state of the world, this limit makes it difficult to empathize with the world at large. Imagining the world as a village of 100 people is not a novel concept but it is something that is important to re-visit once in a while. To view the world as a village of 100 is to reduce the scope of an otherwise incomprehensible scale.
